Gypsum Partition Installation

Material:
Gypsum partitions are constructed using gypsum board or drywall panels.

Purpose:
Gypsum partitions are used to divide interior spaces into separate areas, such as offices, rooms, or compartments.

Installation Process:
The installation process typically involves framing a metal or wooden structure to support the gypsum boards.

Advantages:
Gypsum partitions are lightweight, fire-resistant, and offer good sound insulation properties.

Gypsum Ceiling Installation

Material:
Gypsum ceilings are constructed using gypsum board panels, similar to those used for partitions.

Purpose:
Gypsum ceilings are installed to create a finished and attractive overhead surface in interior spaces.

Installation Process:
Gypsum ceiling installation involves attaching the gypsum board panels to the ceiling's structural framework.

Advantages:
Gypsum ceilings are durable, fire-resistant, and can be designed with various textures and patterns.

Drywall Partition Provider

Design and Planning:
We assist clients in planning the layout and design of partitioned spaces.

Material Selection:
We offer guidance on selecting the appropriate drywall materials and finishes based on factors like sound insulation, fire resistance, and design preferences.

Installation:
Our team construct the drywall partitions using metal or wooden framing and gypsum board or other drywall materials.

Finishing:
After installation, We finish the drywall surfaces by taping, mudding, and sanding joints to create a smooth and paintable surface.

Customization:
Drywall partitions can be customized with paint, wallpaper, or other finishes to match the overall interior design of the space.
